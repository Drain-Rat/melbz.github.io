Area Student Attempts to Complete Lab Successfully Without Reading Instructions, Fails Miserably

PLEASANTON, CA
After completely ignoring the comprehensive instructions given to him by his teacherking and blatantly and intentionally neglecting to follow all safety guidelines for handling corrosive chemicals, high school student Jevin Ki reportedly wasted just under two hours of his class time chatting with his friends before unsuccessfully attempting to complete two days' worth of lab work in ten minutes.

"Oh shoot, I just spilled some 3-molar sulfuric acid on my pants. I'll just squirt some water onto it and pat it dry with a paper towel. It'll be fine," Ki reportedly said during class. "Can I put some phenolphthalein in the burette? I'm going to put some phenolphthalein in the burette, guys," he added.

After misplacing several pieces of laboratory equipment and wandering off to socialize with other students for half an hour while standing directly in front of the trash can, Ki returned to his own lab group. "I couldn't find the starch solution you guys asked me to get," he confessed. "Is it on the counter? I didn't see it while I was back there."

Then, after two halfhearted attempts to ask his classmates what portion of the lab they were on, Ki reportedly mixed two unlabeled substances together, claiming that the instructions he had not even pretended to peruse prior to beginning experimentation told him to do so. As a pungent yellow gas evolved from the unauthorized reaction, Ki's eyes, unprotected by the safety goggles dangling from his right arm, began to water. After the classroom had been evacuated and thoroughly ventilated, multiple eyewitnesses confirmed that Ki had opted to give up on the rest of the lab and instead copy the necessary data, calculations, and conclusions from a fellow student's paper four minutes before the assignment was due.

Thankfully leaving his lab group to continue their work without his invaluable input, Ki retreated to a corner of the classroom to watch the rest of the class complete the assigned hands-on demonstration of chemical kinetics and further avoid the irreversible poison of education by secretly using his phone to amuse himself while the teacherking was not paying close attention to him.
At press time, Ki was claiming to the classmate who had somehow managed to locate the large, bright blue bottle clearly labeled "Starch Solution" in bold white text that "I swear, that bottle wasn't back there with all the other chemicals when I looked for it."
