Test-Taking Tips for High School Students

We all know that strong test scores and grades are hugely important to the students of today. However, it can be very stressful trying to keep up in such a rigorous academic environment. Fortunately, we've compiled a list of helpful test-taking tips and strategies for success:

  • Try not to cram everything at the last minute. You'll do better on the test if you study the material over a period of several weeks instead of the night before. If you feel that you cannot finish the test in the allotted time, an alternative is to curl up in the fetal position and sob quietly into your test booklet instead.

  • Read each problem carefully. Some test questions may be trying to trick you. Be sure to catch every grammar mistake and bring each one to your teacher's attention individually. He or she will appreciate your efforts to improve the test for his or her future students.

  • Take care of yourself. Get enough rest and quality sleep time, eat a good breakfast, and arrive on time with everything you'll need for the test. During the exam, relax and stay calm. If you find yourself getting anxious, just remember that at least you weren't the guy who put phenolphthalein in a burette filled with sodium hydroxide solution.

  • Keep the big picture in mind. If you don't think you're doing well on the test, keep in mind that one bad grade won't matter in the long run. And hey, Eason's probably struggling just as much as you are right now, so it could be worse.
