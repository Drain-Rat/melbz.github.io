US Money Supply Experiencing Unprecedented Inflation

According to new data, the US money supply is currently experiencing rapid inflation. According to financial experts, this trend started in late 2015 and has continued steadily for the past eight months for a number of reasons.

"One thing we've seen with the money supply in the past few weeks especially is an exponential rate of growth," financial analyst John Thomson says. "This increase in volume is due to a number of reasons, most notably to a significant decrease in pressure and simultaneous increase in temperature."

Thomson continues, "Our most recent findings implicate global warming as a long-term cause of this inflation and many students' annual relief at the end of the school year as a more acute contributor."

This inflation has devastated both individual businesses and the American economy at large. To reverse the trend, Thomson recommends instituting year-round schooling and scaling back drastically on carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions. "Theoretically, anything that will lower the temperature and raise the pressure should help curb inflation," he explains.
